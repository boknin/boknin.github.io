Two Monte Carlo methods to approximate π

The quarter-circle has a radius of 1, giving it an area of π/4. Since this is also the proportion of the box that the quarter-circle occupies, this means if we randomly plot points in the box, the expected proportion of points inside the circle is π/4.

The idea behind this approximation comes from Buffon's needle (although I learned about it from here). If we throw a bunch of sticks at parallel lines where the spacing between the lines is the equal to the length of the sticks, then the expected proportion of sticks that cross the lines is 2/π.
